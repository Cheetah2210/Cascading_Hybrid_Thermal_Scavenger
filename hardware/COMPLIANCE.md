# Hardware Compliance & Decommissioning Log: GEN-III CHTS

## 1. System Overview
The Cascading Hybrid Thermal Scavenger (CHTS) GEN-III has been transitioned to a fully solid-state 4-stage architecture. All legacy Magnetohydrodynamic (MHD) components, including ionization chambers and magnetic field containment units, have been decommissioned.

## 2. Decommissioning Summary
* **MHD Status:** DECOMMISSIONED.
* **Hardware Removed:** * Electromagnetic flux induction coils.
    * Ionized plasma containment chasses.
    * High-voltage MHD power conditioning electronics.
* **Replacement:** The system now utilizes a passive 4-stage solid-state cascade (TEG High, TEG Low, Zeotropic, Adsorption).

## 3. Current Architecture Compliance
The system currently complies with the following thermal and electrical safety standards:
* **Thermal Integrity:** Semiconductor junctions (Telluride/Skutterudite) are housed in thermally isolated, vibration-dampened enclosures. 
* **Regulatory Classification:** The system is now classified as a Non-Hazardous Thermal Energy Harvester.
* **Emission Standards:** The system generates zero electromagnetic interference (EMI) as there are no active magnetic field generators.

## 4. Maintenance & Safety Certification
* **Annual Audit:** Annual inspection required for vacuum integrity of the Stage 4 adsorption chiller and the Stage 3 zeotropic condenser loop.
* **Component Disposal:** Spent TEG modules are classified as industrial electronic waste containing heavy metals; disposal must be handled via certified e-waste recycling protocols.

## 5. Official Compliance Declaration
This documentation certifies that the CHTS GEN-III hardware suite is free of volatile MHD fluid-dynamic hazards and is fully operational under the documented solid-state thermal management protocols.
