# Space Adaptation Engineering Guide: GEN-III CHTS

## 1. Vacuum-Rated Architecture
In orbital environments, the absence of atmospheric pressure necessitates the following modifications to the standard CHTS:
- **Hermetic Sealing:** All external interfaces must use metal-to-metal vacuum-grade gaskets.
- **Outgassing Mitigation:** All thermal interface materials (TIM) and adhesives are strictly space-qualified (low-outgassing) to prevent contamination of sensitive optics or sensors.

## 2. Thermal Management in Microgravity
- **Fluid Loop Dynamics:** The Zeotropic stage transitions from pump-driven forced convection to a capillary-driven or surface-tension-driven heat pipe array to ensure effective fluid transport in microgravity.
- **Adsorption Bed Integrity:** Adsorption beds must be reinforced to prevent structural degradation due to vacuum-induced pressure differentials.

## 3. Radiator Integration
- **Heat Rejection:** The CHTS waste heat rejection system is integrated directly into the craft’s primary thermal bus. The system must utilize high-emissivity radiator panels to dump waste thermal energy into the deep space heat sink.

## 4. Radiation Hardening
- **Semiconductor Shielding:** All controller and telemetry electronics must be housed in an aluminum enclosure with a minimum thickness of 2.5mm to shield against ionizing space radiation.
